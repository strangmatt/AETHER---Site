Psilocybin

4-Phosphoryloxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine

Overview

Psilocybin is a naturally occurring psychedelic prodrug found in over 200 species of fungi, commonly known as "magic mushrooms." Once ingested, it is converted in the body to psilocin, which acts primarily as a serotonin 5-HT2A receptor agonist, producing profound alterations in perception, mood, and cognition.

History

Psilocybin mushrooms have been used ceremonially for thousands of years across Mesoamerican cultures, particularly by the Mazatec people of Oaxaca, Mexico. Spanish colonizers documented their ritual use in the 16th century. In 1958, Swiss chemist Albert Hofmann — who also synthesized LSD — first isolated and identified psilocybin and psilocin from Psilocybe mexicana.

Effects

Effects typically begin 20–60 minutes after ingestion and last 4–6 hours. Common experiences include visual and auditory hallucinations, synesthesia, altered sense of time, deep introspection, emotional amplification, and feelings of interconnectedness. At higher doses, users may experience ego dissolution — a temporary loss of the sense of self.

Research

Clinical trials at Johns Hopkins University, NYU, and Imperial College London have demonstrated significant therapeutic promise. Studies show psilocybin-assisted therapy produces rapid and lasting reductions in treatment-resistant depression, end-of-life anxiety, and tobacco addiction. The FDA has granted psilocybin "Breakthrough Therapy" designation for major depressive disorder.

Legal Status

Psilocybin is classified as a Schedule I controlled substance under US federal law. However, Ann Arbor, Michigan decriminalized natural psychedelics including psilocybin mushrooms in 2020. Oregon became the first state to legalize regulated therapeutic use in 2020, with licensed service centers operating since 2023. Colorado followed in 2022.

Harm Reduction

Set and setting — mindset and environment — are the most significant factors influencing the quality of a psilocybin experience. Having a trusted, sober guide present is strongly recommended. Avoid combining with lithium, MAOIs, or stimulants. Those with a personal or family history of psychosis or schizophrenia should avoid use.
