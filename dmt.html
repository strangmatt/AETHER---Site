DMT

N,N-Dimethyltryptamine

Overview

N,N-Dimethyltryptamine (DMT) is a naturally occurring tryptamine psychedelic found in many plants and animals, and produced endogenously in the human body. It acts as a serotonin 5-HT2A receptor agonist and produces one of the most intense psychedelic experiences known, typically lasting only 5–20 minutes when smoked or vaporized.

History

DMT was first synthesized by Canadian chemist Richard Manske in 1931. Its psychoactive properties were discovered by Hungarian chemist Stephen Szara in 1956. Indigenous Amazonian peoples have used DMT-containing plant brews for centuries in spiritual and healing ceremonies. When combined with an MAOI such as harmaline, it becomes orally active as ayahuasca.

Effects

When vaporized, effects onset within seconds and resolve within 15–20 minutes. Users commonly report vivid geometric visuals, encounters with perceived entities, travel through otherworldly dimensions, and a profound sense of entering an alternate reality. The experience is often described as more real than ordinary consciousness. Oral DMT via ayahuasca lasts 4–6 hours.

Research

Dr. Rick Strassman conducted the first federally approved psychedelic research in decades at the University of New Mexico in the 1990s, administering IV DMT to human volunteers and documenting entity contact experiences. Current research explores DMT's role in near-death experiences, consciousness, sigma-1 receptor activation, and neuroplasticity.

Legal Status

DMT is classified as a Schedule I controlled substance under US federal law. It is decriminalized in Ann Arbor, Michigan. Ayahuasca use by certain religious organizations has received legal protection under the Religious Freedom Restoration Act in the United States.

Harm Reduction

Due to its intensity, DMT is best approached with experience and preparation. Never use alone. Do not combine with MAOIs unless using the oral ayahuasca preparation with proper guidance. Ensure a physically safe environment as motor control is significantly impaired during the experience. Those with cardiovascular conditions or psychiatric history should consult a physician.
