Mescaline

3,4,5-Trimethoxyphenethylamine

Overview

Mescaline is a naturally occurring phenethylamine psychedelic found primarily in peyote (Lophophora williamsii), San Pedro cactus (Echinopsis pachanoi), and Peruvian torch cactus (Echinopsis peruviana). It acts as a serotonin 5-HT2A receptor agonist and produces a long-duration psychedelic experience characterized by vivid color enhancement and deep introspection.

History

Peyote has been used in Indigenous spiritual ceremonies for over 5,000 years, making mescaline one of the oldest known psychedelics in human use. German chemist Arthur Heffter isolated mescaline from peyote in 1897. Ernst Späth first synthesized it in 1919. Aldous Huxley famously documented his mescaline experience in "The Doors of Perception" (1954).

Effects

Effects onset 1–2 hours after ingestion and last 10–12 hours, making it one of the longest-acting classic psychedelics. The experience is characterized by vivid, colorful visual hallucinations, heightened sensory perception, profound empathy and emotional depth, and strong introspective clarity. The headspace is often described as clearer and more grounded than other psychedelics.

Research

A major 2021 survey study published in ACS Pharmacology & Translational Science found that mescaline use was associated with significant improvements in depression, anxiety, and PTSD, with effects comparable to psilocybin. Indigenous ceremonial use provides a rich ethnobotanical record. Clinical research is emerging, though less advanced than psilocybin or MDMA studies.

Legal Status

Mescaline is classified as a Schedule I controlled substance under US federal law. Peyote use is legally protected for members of the Native American Church. Ann Arbor, Michigan has decriminalized natural psychedelics including mescaline. San Pedro cactus is legal to possess in most jurisdictions as an ornamental plant.

Harm Reduction

Due to its long duration, plan for a full day with no obligations. Nausea during the come-up is common, especially with peyote — having an empty stomach reduces this. Do not combine with MAOIs, lithium, or stimulants. Ensure access to a safe, comfortable environment and a trusted person. Cardiac stress is possible at high doses in those with heart conditions.
