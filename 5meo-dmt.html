5-MeO-DMT

5-Methoxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine

Overview

5-Methoxy-N,N-dimethyltryptamine (5-MeO-DMT) is a potent naturally occurring psychedelic tryptamine found in the venom of the Sonoran Desert toad (Bufo alvarius) and in numerous plant species. It is considered one of the most powerful psychoactive substances known, producing states of complete ego dissolution and non-dual awareness.

History

5-MeO-DMT was first synthesized in 1936 by Toshio Hoshino and Kenya Shimodaira. Its presence in Bufo alvarius venom was identified by Albert Most in 1984. Indigenous Amazonian shamans have long used 5-MeO-DMT-containing plant preparations such as yopo and vilca in ceremonial contexts for healing and divination.

Effects

The experience is typically non-visual and intensely felt rather than seen. Users describe complete dissolution of the sense of self, merging with an undifferentiated field of pure consciousness, overwhelming feelings of love and unity, and encounters with what many describe as "the void" or the ground of being. Effects last 15–45 minutes when vaporized.

Research

A landmark 2019 study published in Frontiers in Psychiatry found that a single dose of synthetic 5-MeO-DMT produced rapid and sustained reductions in anxiety and depression, with 80% of participants reporting improvements. Ongoing research is examining its potential for PTSD, addiction, and treatment-resistant depression. Johns Hopkins has an active research program.

Legal Status

5-MeO-DMT is classified as a Schedule I controlled substance under US federal law. It is decriminalized in Ann Arbor, Michigan. The collection of Bufo alvarius toad venom is regulated in some states and Mexico. Synthetic 5-MeO-DMT is increasingly used in research and retreat settings in jurisdictions where it is not scheduled.

Harm Reduction

5-MeO-DMT should never be used alone. A trained, sober guide is essential. It is absolutely contraindicated with MAOIs — the combination can be fatal. Those with a history of cardiac conditions, psychosis, or significant trauma should proceed with extreme caution. Integration support after the experience is strongly recommended.
